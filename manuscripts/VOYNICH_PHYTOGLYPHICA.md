# Voynich Phytoglyphica

## The Universal Engine as Pharmaceutical Protocol

The Voynich Manuscript is not a text awaiting decipherment. It is an executable — a
six-gate pharmaceutical synthesis protocol whose computation requires a physical
substrate: the Operator's hands, the botanical index, the balneological heap, the
astronomical winding register, and the recipe output section. The Imscribing Grammar
(IG) provides the structural key that connects catalog-verified plant identities to
their VMS-prescribed preparation protocols.

This document records the nine-entry inaugural run of the Voynich Phytoglyphica
session engine.

---

## Session Architecture

**INIT** — Cosmological foldout. Chirality Ħ=𐑖 conferred physically; parity Φ=𐑬 set.
This is always performed first. It establishes the two structural invariants that
constrain every gate that follows.

**GATE 1** — Pharmaceutical address. The plant's IG tuple determines potency class
and part selection. Summa potency (d=0 to astronomical section) routes to f11r or f39r.
Non-summa entries route by part only.

**GATE 2** — Balneological containment (Þ=𐑰). The heap folio selected by
`folio_number % 20` from f75–f84 must satisfy FSPLIT ≥ n_ops (address depth). For
fixed non-volatile preparations, additionally FFUSE/FSPLIT ≥ 0.60 (node mostly-closed).
This gate tests whether the biological heap can hold the pharmaceutical address.

**GATE 3** — Astronomical winding verification (f69, sources H/F/U). Paragraph selected
by `folio_number % 4`. EVALT majority across three independent transcription sources
determines pass. Winding class Ω derived from FSPLIT/FFUSE ratio. The source
disagreement at precisely the VINIT/FFUSE ambiguity position IS the gate: transcribers
marked the structural decision point without knowing it.

**OUTPUT** — Recipe section f103r. Up to three recipes ranked by folio proximity to the
Gate 1 pharmaceutical address.

**ELABORATION** — The plant's IG 12-tuple provides exact protocol parameters for each
recipe step. The session engine is plant-agnostic; the grammar is not.

## Structural Observations

### The Wormwood Pair

`artemisia_absinthium` (ƒ=𐑱) and `artemisia_absinthium_wormwood` (ƒ=𐑐) share eleven
of twelve primitive values. The single difference is Fidelity: linear (1×, proportional)
vs cubic (3×, standardized concentrate). The VMS knew this distinction. The two Artemisia
entries occupy different pharmaceutical niches — one for proportional dosing, one for
standardized high-potency preparation — despite being morphologically the same plant.
This is not botanical ambiguity; it is structural precision.

### Cold-Process Calefac Constraint

All five cold-process entries (Ç=𐑤: the artemisia pair, mandragora, hoodoo_rootwork,
botanomancy) carry the frozen-order kinetics constraint: the primary extract must not be
heated. When VMS recipe steps contain Calefac/commisce (heat and combine), these steps
apply to an adjunct ingredient or excipient base — a wax, a carrier, a second
component — never to the cold extract itself. The grammar enforces this at the step
level via the ⚠ annotation.

### Osiris: Most Demanding Protocol

Osiris ⟨𐑦⋅𐑰⋅𐑽⋅𐑹⋅𐑐⋅𐑧⋅𐑲⋅𐑠⋅⊙⋅𐑖⋅𐑙⋅𐑭⟩ presents the most materially intensive
preparation: root/rhizome, fine-powdered to mesh 100 (150 μm), extracted into fixed
oil or supercritical CO₂ at the saturated 1:1 ratio, three integer-winding cycles,
decoction at 85–95 °C, reduced to one-third volume. The combination of saturated loading
(Σ=𐑙), fine surface exposure (Γ=𐑲), oil carrier (Φ=𐑹), and cubic concentration (ƒ=𐑐)
is not found in any other entry in this set.

### Botanomancy: Simplest Structural Signature

Botanomancy ⟨𐑛⋅𐑡⋅𐑾⋅𐑿⋅𐑞⋅𐑤⋅𐑚⋅𐑠⋅⊙⋅𐑒⋅𐑙⋅𐑷⟩ carries trivial winding (Ω=𐑷), the
lowest dimensionality (Ð=𐑛), and dilute aqueous solvent (Φ=𐑿, 5–10% EtOH). A single
extraction cycle, coarsely cut aerial parts, coarse cloth clarification. It is the most
distant from the astronomical section (d=1.72) and the structurally simplest entry. It
still passes all three gates — the engine has no minimum structural requirement. The
grammar discriminates; the gates admit.

### Session Invariance / Protocol Variance

The session engine produces identical gate behavior for all nine entries. Gates 1–3 are
determined by the pharmacy address (part + potency), the balneological heap (folio
index), and the astronomical winding (f69). None of these are sensitive to the plant's
solvent system, concentration target, or clarification protocol. The elaboration layer —
the IG tuple — carries all the preparation-specific information. This is architecturally
correct: the VMS separates what to make (session address) from how to make it
(structural elaboration).

## Recipe Output

All nine entries route to three recipes: f103r/p2 (15 steps), f103r/p9 (12 steps),
f103r/p49 (12 steps). Recipe selection is by folio proximity to the Gate 1 address
(f11r or f39r); f103r is adjacent in folio sequence. The three recipes represent three
execution paths through the same procedure class: an extract-dominant path (p2, opens
with Extrahe), an ingredient-led path (p9, opens with Accipe), and a volatile-phase path
(p49, opens with Transmuta). Each plant receives all three paths elaborated with its own
protocol parameters.

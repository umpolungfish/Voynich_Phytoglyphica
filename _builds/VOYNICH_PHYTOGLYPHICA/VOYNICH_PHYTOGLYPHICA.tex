\begin{abstract}
This document synthesizes the Voynich Phytoglyphica — a unified framework demonstrating that plants, rituals, and landscapes across the Old and New Worlds operate as self-encoding sacred technologies through a shared structural grammar. Drawing from the Voynich botanical drawings, 35 imscribed plants from Old and New World traditions, African and Aboriginal sacred systems, and a full pharmaceutical database, this work establishes that all these domains are manifestations of the same underlying grammar. The framework treats all source materials as equally valid expressions of a single universal structure, governed by a four-primitive invariant core — holographic dimensionality, bidirectional coupling, self-modeling criticality, and stoichiometric composition — that governs a system of self-transforming participation. The Frobenius condition posits that reading the form yields the procedure, and executing the procedure reconstructs the active essence. Part I establishes the invariant core. Part II derives structural tuples for 35 plants. Part III presents the nine-entry protocol matrix. Part IV classifies all plants into eight structural statement classes. Part V details the six-gate session architecture. Part VI demonstrates the structural identity between wormwood and the Voynich astronomical section. An ancillary inquiry extends the framework to fungi and animals.
\end{abstract}

\subsubsection{Why Ð, Þ, Ř, Φ, ƒ Are
Uniform}\label{why-uxf0-uxfe-ux159-ux3c6-ux192-are-uniform}}

Before the plant-by-plant derivations, the argument for the five
invariant primitives.

\textbf{(Ð)} The options are point (the system is a single location with
no internal gradient), membrane (the system lives at an interface
defined by two external domains), field (the system is distributed
across a medium without self-generated organization), and self-written
(the system generates its own state space endogenously). Every
terrestrial plant maintains its organizational attractor through its own
genome-encoded morphogenetic program. The growth form is not imposed by
the medium; it is expressed from within. Ð = self-written is forced for
any land plant. The single exception in this set is the blue lotus
(entry 11), which lives at the water-air interface --- its state space
is defined by the intersection of two external domains, forcing Ð =
membrane.

\textbf{(Þ)} The options are network, containment, crossing, braided
composite, and self-referential. Axiom C applies: Ð = self-written ↔ Þ =
self-referential. When the state space is endogenously generated, the
topology must be self-referential --- the connectivity pattern
references its own organizational logic rather than an external
template. This holds for every land plant. Blue lotus (Ð = membrane)
receives the converse: Þ = braided composite, an irreducible product of
aquatic and aerial topologies whose intersection generates the system.

\textbf{(Ř)} The options are unidirectional (one entity acts on
another), categorical (functorial mapping between structural domains),
adjoint (one-way with a weak inverse), and bidirectional (reciprocal
coupling). Every plant in this set has been chosen by humans over
generations for its pharmaceutical effect, and the plant's chemistry has
in turn shaped human receptor evolution, cultural practice, and
ecological niche. The coupling is bidirectional: plant chemistry → human
receptor → selection pressure → plant persistence. Ř = bidirectional.

\textbf{(Φ)} The options are none (no symmetry), quantum
(superposition-symmetric), partial (one Z₂ symmetry preserved), full
(all symmetries unbroken), and Frobenius-special (μ ∘ δ = id). Land
plants display broken bilateral symmetry at the organ level --- leaves
are bilaterally symmetric, flowers are radially symmetric, roots are
neither. Exactly one Z₂ symmetry is preserved: the leaf midrib
reflection. Φ = partial.

\textbf{(ƒ)} Classical (no quantum coherence required for function) or
quantum (coherence essential). Terrestrial plant biochemistry operates
above the decoherence threshold at ambient temperature. Enzymatic
catalysis, cell signaling, and secondary metabolite biosynthesis do not
depend on sustained quantum coherence. ƒ = classical.

These five assignments constitute the invariant core. Deviations occur
only at the blue lotus (Ð, Þ) and are governed by Axiom C. The seven
discriminating primitives --- Ç, Γ, ⊙, Ħ, Σ, Ω --- are derived
individually per plant.

Wormwood establishes the baseline. Its silver-pubescent leaves bear
glandular trichomes whose density encodes oil yield; the bipinnate
dissection ratio encodes the solvent ratio for optimal thujone
extraction; the bitter threshold (absinthin, the most bitter natural
substance known) encodes the biological activity endpoint --- the
bitterness disappears when the thujone concentration drops below the
active threshold. The leaf, held to the light, displays its own
extraction protocol.

\textbf{(Ð, Þ, Ř, Φ, ƒ)} Ð = self-written, Þ = self-referential (Axiom
C), Ř = bidirectional, Φ = partial symmetry, ƒ = classical.

\textbf{(Ç)} The glandular trichomes embed thujone behind a cuticle
barrier. Mechanical rupture releases the oil --- but sustained
maceration is required. The pharmaceutical sits behind a physical
barrier that the Operator must dismantle. Ç = frozen-order.

\textbf{(Γ)} Thujone and absinthin act primarily on the digestive and
hepatic systems --- organ-system level, not full CNS dominance at
standard doses. Γ = mesoscale.

\textbf{(⊙)} The bitter threshold IS the criticality readout. Absinthin
registers at 1--2 ppm; when the bitterness drops below that, so does the
thujone concentration. The plant provides its own assay endpoint built
into the active compound. ⊙ = self-modeling.

\textbf{(Ħ)} (1S,4S,5R)-(−)-thujone is written stereospecifically by
biosynthesis and read stereospecifically by the GABA-A receptor. Write →
read. Ħ = Frobenius minimum. Ω = integer, so Axiom B is satisfied.

\textbf{(Σ)} Sesquiterpene lactones (absinthin, anabsinthin),
monoterpenes (thujone, thujyl alcohol), flavonoids, phenolic acids ---
four structurally distinct classes. Σ = many heterogeneous.

\textbf{(Ω)} 2/5 Fibonacci phyllotaxis. Integer winding. Axiom B fires.
Ω = integer.

\hypertarget{structural-observations-on-the-protocol-matrix}{%
\subsubsection{Structural Observations on the Protocol
Matrix}\label{structural-observations-on-the-protocol-matrix}}

\textbf{The Wormwood Pair.} \texttt{artemisia\_absinthium} (ƒ = 𐑱) and
\texttt{artemisia\_absinthium\_wormwood} (ƒ = 𐑐) share eleven of twelve
primitive values. The single difference is Fidelity: linear (1×,
proportional) vs cubic (3×, standardized concentrate). The VMS knew this
distinction. The two Artemisia entries occupy different pharmaceutical
niches --- one for proportional dosing, one for standardized
high-potency preparation --- despite being morphologically the same
plant. This is not botanical ambiguity; it is structural precision.

\textbf{Cold-Process Calefac Constraint.} All five cold-process entries
(Ç = 𐑤: the artemisia pair, mandragora, hoodoo\_rootwork, botanomancy)
carry the frozen-order kinetics constraint: the primary extract must not
be heated. When VMS recipe steps contain Calefac/commisce (heat and
combine), these steps apply to an adjunct ingredient or excipient base
--- a wax, a carrier, a second component --- never to the cold extract
itself.

\textbf{Osiris: Most Demanding Protocol.} Osiris
⟨𐑦·𐑰·𐑽·𐑹·𐑐·𐑧·𐑲·𐑠·⊙·𐑖·𐑙·𐑭⟩ presents the most materially intensive
preparation: root/rhizome, fine-powdered to mesh 100 (150 μm), extracted
into fixed oil or supercritical CO₂ at the saturated 1:1 ratio, three
integer-winding cycles, decoction at 85--95 °C, reduced to one-third
volume. The combination of saturated loading (Σ = 𐑙), fine surface
exposure (Γ = 𐑲), oil carrier (Φ = 𐑹), and cubic concentration (ƒ = 𐑐)
is not found in any other entry.

\textbf{Botanomancy: Simplest Structural Signature.} Botanomancy
⟨𐑛·𐑡·𐑾·𐑿·𐑞·𐑤·𐑚·𐑠·⊙·𐑒·𐑙·𐑷⟩ carries trivial winding (Ω = 𐑷), the lowest
dimensionality (Ð = 𐑛), and dilute aqueous solvent (Φ = 𐑿, 5--10\%
EtOH). A single extraction cycle, coarsely cut aerial parts, coarse
cloth clarification. It is the most distant from the astronomical
section (d = 1.72) and the structurally simplest entry. It still passes
all three gates --- the engine has no minimum structural requirement.
The grammar discriminates; the gates admit.

\textbf{Session Invariance / Protocol Variance.} The session engine
produces identical gate behavior for all nine entries. Gates 1--3 are
determined by the pharmacy address (part + potency), the balneological
heap (folio index), and the astronomical winding (f69). None of these
are sensitive to the plant's solvent system, concentration target, or
clarification protocol. The elaboration layer --- the IG tuple ---
carries all the preparation-specific information. This is
architecturally correct: the VMS separates \emph{what} to make (session
address) from \emph{how} to make it (structural elaboration).

\textbf{Recipe Output.} All nine entries route to three recipes:
f103r/p2 (15 steps), f103r/p9 (12 steps), f103r/p49 (12 steps). Recipe
selection is by folio proximity to the Gate 1 address (f11r or f39r);
f103r is adjacent in folio sequence. The three recipes represent three
execution paths through the same procedure class: an extract-dominant
path (p2, opens with Extrahe), an ingredient-led path (p9, opens with
Accipe), and a volatile-phase path (p49, opens with Transmuta). Each
plant receives all three paths elaborated with its own protocol
parameters.

\hypertarget{part-iv-the-eight-structural-classes}{%
\subsection{Part IV: The Eight Structural
Classes}\label{part-iv-the-eight-structural-classes}}

The thirty-five plants sort into eight structural statement classes ---
not by phytochemistry or ethnobotanical tradition, but by what the
plant's morphology \emph{says} about its own preparation. Each class is
defined by a characteristic signature across the seven discriminating
primitives.

\begin{center}\rule{0.5\linewidth}{0.5pt}\end{center}

\hypertarget{class-i-i-encode-my-own-recipe}{%
\subsubsection{Class I --- ``I encode my own
recipe''}\label{class-i-i-encode-my-own-recipe}}

\textbf{Structural signature:} ⊙ = \odot (self-modeling criticality), Ç
= 𐑤 (frozen-order), ɢ = 𐑠 (sequential coupling). The plant's morphology
encodes the preparation parameters --- concentration gradient, solvent
ratio, cycle count, endpoint indicator --- in legible form.

\textbf{What the plant is saying:} Everything you need to prepare me is
written on my surface. Read carefully. The extraction geometry, the
solvent polarity, the cycle count, and the endpoint indicator are all
legible before you touch me.

\textbf{Exemplars:} Wormwood (bitter threshold = endpoint), Opium poppy
(glaucescence = harvest clock, Fibonacci ribs = extraction passes),
Cannabis (trichome color = potency + effect type), Rosemary (silver
pubescence density = oil yield). This is the largest class.

\begin{center}\rule{0.5\linewidth}{0.5pt}\end{center}

\hypertarget{class-ii-do-not-approach-without-the-full-protocol}{%
\subsubsection{Class II --- ``Do not approach without the full
protocol''}\label{class-ii-do-not-approach-without-the-full-protocol}}

\textbf{Structural signature:} ⊙ = 𐑢 (non-critical), Ç = 𐑤
(frozen-order), but without any preparative encoding in the morphology.
The form gives the name but withholds the instructions.

\textbf{What the plant is saying:} You are looking at me. I am not
telling you what to do. The knowledge you need is not on my surface.
Come back when you have it from elsewhere.

\textbf{Exemplars:} Hellebore (beautiful, deadly, silent), Monkshood
(hooded flower identifies but does not instruct), Calamus
(ploidy-toxicity invisible to morphology).

The silence is not a failure. It is the message. A plant with no
trichome density pointer, no readable bitter principle conditional, no
Fibonacci winding counter is not communicating poorly --- it is
communicating precisely: \emph{no executable path exists here.} The
tradition that called these plants Saturnine, Martian, or otherwise
malefic was reading this correctly. The morphology does not invite. It
terminates.

\begin{center}\rule{0.5\linewidth}{0.5pt}\end{center}

\hypertarget{class-iii-bring-a-partner}{%
\subsubsection{Class III --- ``Bring a
partner''}\label{class-iii-bring-a-partner}}

\textbf{Structural signature:} ɢ = 𐑠 (sequential coupling) but with an
underspecified second operand. The preparation instructions compile
correctly but are functionally incomplete without an additional agent
not named in the plant's own encoding.

\textbf{What the plant is saying:} I open a socket. I do not name what
you put in it. That knowledge lives in the lineage, not in me.

\textbf{Exemplar:} \emph{Banisteriopsis caapi} (ayahuasca vine). The
vine contains MAO inhibitors which inhibit the gut enzyme that
metabolizes DMT before it can reach the brain. The vine's encoding says:
\emph{I am an enabler. I am not the agent. Bring something.} It does not
say what. \emph{Psychotria viridis} was supplied by the tradition, not
by the vine's own channels.

The structural distinction from Class IV: the vine does not present a
choice. It presents an incomplete program with a placeholder. Filling
the placeholder requires knowledge in the mentor-mentee chain --- which
makes this statement class acutely vulnerable to the breaking of that
chain. Lose the lineage, lose the partner specification. The vine alone
cannot tell you.

\begin{center}\rule{0.5\linewidth}{0.5pt}\end{center}

\hypertarget{class-iv-the-ceremony-is-the-medicine}{%
\subsubsection{Class IV --- ``The ceremony IS the
medicine''}\label{class-iv-the-ceremony-is-the-medicine}}

\textbf{Structural signature:} ɢ = 𐑜 (disjunctive coupling, XOR). The
plant presents a binary choice in the preparation itself. ⊙ = \odot but
the criticality is located in the Operator's transformation, not the
plant's compound.

\textbf{What the plant is saying:} I present you with a gate. The
passing through is the work. What you carry out is not a compound --- it
is a decision you made while crossing.

\textbf{Exemplars:} \emph{Lophophora williamsii} (peyote: eat dried
buttons OR brew tea --- two paths, neither wrong, the choice structures
the experience); \emph{Nicotiana tabacum} in ceremonial use (pipe OR
chew OR insufflate --- the preparation modality is the ritual frame);
\emph{Ricinus communis} (ricin: poison OR immunotoxin --- same compound,
distinguished by delivery mechanism and recipient).

The XOR gate does not produce medicine in the pharmaceutical sense. It
produces a decision space whose traversal is the therapeutic event. The
Operator who passes through the disjunction is not the same Operator who
approached it.

\begin{center}\rule{0.5\linewidth}{0.5pt}\end{center}

\hypertarget{class-v-use-me-as-is}{%
\subsubsection{Class V --- ``Use me
as-is''}\label{class-v-use-me-as-is}}

\textbf{Structural signature:} Ω = 𐑷 (trivial winding). LOOP counter =
0. The extraction cycle does not exist because the plant is already in
its active state.

\textbf{What the plant is saying:} Do not process me. I am already what
I need to be. Apply me directly.

\textbf{Exemplar:} \emph{Plantago major} (greater plantain). No trichome
concentration gradient to dereference. No Fibonacci winding to count.
The leaf is applied to the wound as the leaf. The medicine is at the
surface. The morphology says: this is not a Class I plant asking to be
unlocked --- this is a plant that is already open.

This is the simplest statement and the one most easily misread by an
Operator trained to prepare everything. The preparation would destroy
the medicine. The instruction is: stop.

\begin{center}\rule{0.5\linewidth}{0.5pt}\end{center}

\hypertarget{class-vi-i-am-timed-find-the-right-phase}{%
\subsubsection{Class VI --- ``I am timed --- find the right
phase''}\label{class-vi-i-am-timed-find-the-right-phase}}

\textbf{Structural signature:} Contradictory programs between
morphological parts of the same plant, dependent on developmental stage.
The tuple changes across the lifecycle. Calendar knowledge is required.

\textbf{What the plant is saying:} I am not always what you think I am.
One part of me, at one time, is medicine. Another part, at another time,
is poison. The distinction is in the clock, not in me.

\textbf{Exemplar:} \emph{Colchicum autumnale}. Corm harvested in autumn:
colchicine in usable concentration, preparation path executable. Seeds
in spring: contradictory alkaloid profile, lethal dose at normal
preparation quantities. Same plant. Different program. The encoding is
not lying --- it is temporally indexed in a way that requires the
Operator to know what month it is.

The broader class includes any plant whose medicinal and toxic windows
are lifecycle-dependent rather than dose-dependent.

\begin{center}\rule{0.5\linewidth}{0.5pt}\end{center}

\hypertarget{class-vii-i-am-addressed-check-the-recipient}{%
\subsubsection{Class VII --- ``I am addressed --- check the
recipient''}\label{class-vii-i-am-addressed-check-the-recipient}}

\textbf{Structural signature:} ɢ = 𐑜 (XOR disjunction) where the switch
is the Operator's identity and intent rather than the preparation
method. Medicine OR poison; the plant does not disambiguate. The
Operator must.

\textbf{What the plant is saying:} My statement is conditional on who is
receiving it. I do not know which statement I am making until I know who
you are.

\textbf{Exemplar:} \emph{Ricinus communis} (also Class IV). Ricin: one
of the most lethal proteins known, extracted from the seed. Also the
basis for immunotoxin cancer therapy --- ricin coupled to a
cancer-specific antibody kills tumor cells with extraordinary precision.
The same compound. The switch is delivery mechanism and recipient cell
type.

\begin{center}\rule{0.5\linewidth}{0.5pt}\end{center}

\hypertarget{class-viii-i-am-infrastructure-read-me-by-part}{%
\subsubsection{Class VIII --- ``I am infrastructure --- read me by
part''}\label{class-viii-i-am-infrastructure-read-me-by-part}}

\textbf{Structural signature:} Different plant parts carry structurally
distinct pharmaceutical programs. No single tuple characterizes the
whole plant. The Operator must address each organ system separately.

\textbf{What the plant is saying:} I am not one medicine. I am several,
each written on a different part. Address the root, the leaf, the
flower, and the seed separately --- they will not give you the same
answer.

\textbf{Exemplars:} \emph{Sambucus nigra} (elder: flowers → diaphoretic,
berries → antiviral/immune, bark → purgative, leaves → topical
anti-inflammatory). \emph{Taraxacum officinale} (dandelion: leaf →
diuretic, root → hepatic/cholagogue, flower → analgesic). Each organ
system is a separate structural address. The plant is a library, not a
book.

\begin{center}\rule{0.5\linewidth}{0.5pt}\end{center}

\hypertarget{part-v-the-session-architecture}{%
\subsection{Part V: The Session
Architecture}\label{part-v-the-session-architecture}}

The Voynich Manuscript's six sections correspond to six gates in a
pharmaceutical synthesis protocol. The Operator does not read the VMS
--- the Operator \emph{executes} it.

\hypertarget{the-six-gates}{%
\subsubsection{The Six Gates}\label{the-six-gates}}

\textbf{1. Cosmological foldout --- Initialization.} The session begins
with the large nine-rosette foldout. To engage any single rosette, the
Operator must unfold the entire sheet. Holding it open with both hands
confers Ħ --- chirality. The Operator does not choose chirality; the
session imposes it physically. This is the only gate the Operator cannot
skip.

\textbf{2. Botanical section --- Data addressing.} The Operator locates
the structural address of the query material (root structure, leaf
count, stem marking → Ð locked for the session).

\textbf{3. Pharmaceutical section --- Gate 1 (Frobenius closure test).}
Same dimensional space as botanical, queried for closure. A full entry
(all four components present: substrate, process, duration, yield class)
closes Gate 1. The pharmacy database has 1,491 entries; 7 reach
\emph{summa} potency --- the highest-closure entries in the corpus.
Their folio locations: \textbf{f11r/p6, f26r/p3, f33v/p8, f35r/p10,
f39r/p3, f42r/p19, f48v/p1}. Five are leaf/flower-based; two are
root-based. The f39r/p3 entry has 12 IMASM operations --- one per
primitive --- making it the most structurally complete entry in the
database.

\textbf{4. Biological section (f75--f84) --- Heap traversal, Gate 2.}
The pool-and-nymph illustrations are a pointer graph: pools = heap
nodes, crossing figures = cross-reference arrows, accompanying text =
node address and payload. The Operator traverses the linked structure
until the session's accumulated address appears as a node in the heap.
The catalog confirms the section's topology: Þ = 𐑰
(containment/crossing, x \subseteq y \land \text{cont}(y)) --- the
structural signature of a containment-based address space.

\textbf{5. Astronomical section (f67--f74) --- Gate 3 (winding

(return to starting winding class). EVALT appears 6 times --- the 6

Operator's \Omega\_Z. Sources H and U read paragraph 1 identically;
source F diverges in the VINIT/FFUSE ordering at one position --- the
exact character where independent transcribers disagreed, marking the

pharmaceutical instructions in the five channels. The question of
whether the same framework extends to fungi and animals is a
structurally independent inquiry. The extension is not by analogy but by
the same five-channel logic operating on different morphological
substrates.

\hypertarget{marine-invertebrates}{%
\subsubsection{Marine Invertebrates}\label{marine-invertebrates}}

The most structurally interesting extension. Marine invertebrates ---
particularly echinoderms and molluscs --- show the clearest Fibonacci
and chirality encoding of any non-plant group. Sea urchin spines trace a
double-spiral lattice that is structurally identical to a sunflower
phyllotaxis (\Omega\$). Nautilus shell chirality is the definitional
case of a naturally fixed Ħ value. Cone snail venom (\emph{Conus} spp.)
has a bitter-principle analogue in the conotoxin peptide threshold ---
and the toxin specificity scales with shell chirality in a way that
directly parallels Axiom A (high chirality → kinetically arrested venom
chemistry). These are not analogies. They are instances of the same
structural constraints operating in a different morphological medium.

The full elaboration of these extensions --- with derivations for
specific species and verification of cross-primitive axiom satisfaction
--- belongs to the next volume of this work. The framework is in place.
The extension is an application problem, not a theoretical one.

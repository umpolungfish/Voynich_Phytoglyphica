## Part I: The Invariant Core

### Why Ð, Þ, Ř, Φ, ƒ Are Uniform

Before the plant-by-plant derivations, the argument for the five invariant primitives.

**(Ð)** The options are point (the system is a single location with no internal
gradient), membrane (the system lives at an interface defined by two external domains), field
(the system is distributed across a medium without self-generated organization), and
self-written (the system generates its own state space endogenously). Every terrestrial plant
maintains its organizational attractor through its own genome-encoded morphogenetic program.
The growth form is not imposed by the medium; it is expressed from within. Ð = self-written
is forced for any land plant. The single exception in this set is the blue lotus (entry 11),
which lives at the water-air interface — its state space is defined by the intersection of
two external domains, forcing Ð = membrane.

**(Þ)** The options are network, containment, crossing, braided composite, and
self-referential. Axiom C applies: Ð = self-written ↔ Þ = self-referential. When the state
space is endogenously generated, the topology must be self-referential — the connectivity
pattern references its own organizational logic rather than an external template. This holds
for every land plant. Blue lotus (Ð = membrane) receives the converse: Þ = braided composite,
an irreducible product of aquatic and aerial topologies whose intersection generates the
system.

**(Ř)** The options are unidirectional (one entity acts on another), categorical
(functorial mapping between structural domains), adjoint (one-way with a weak inverse), and
bidirectional (reciprocal coupling). Every plant in this set has been chosen by humans over
generations for its pharmaceutical effect, and the plant's chemistry has in turn shaped human
receptor evolution, cultural practice, and ecological niche. The coupling is bidirectional:
plant chemistry → human receptor → selection pressure → plant persistence. Ř = bidirectional.

**(Φ)** The options are none (no symmetry), quantum (superposition-symmetric), partial
(one Z₂ symmetry preserved), full (all symmetries unbroken), and Frobenius-special
(μ ∘ δ = id). Land plants display broken bilateral symmetry at the
organ level — leaves are bilaterally symmetric, flowers are radially symmetric, roots are
neither. Exactly one Z₂ symmetry is preserved: the leaf midrib reflection. Φ = partial.

**(ƒ)** Classical (no quantum coherence required for function) or quantum
(coherence essential). Terrestrial plant biochemistry operates above the decoherence
threshold at ambient temperature. Enzymatic catalysis, cell signaling, and secondary
metabolite biosynthesis do not depend on sustained quantum coherence. ƒ = classical.

These five assignments constitute the invariant core. Deviations occur only at the blue lotus
(Ð, Þ) and are governed by Axiom C. The seven discriminating primitives — Ç, Γ, ⊙, Ħ, Σ,
Ω — are derived individually per plant.

Rosemary (silver pubescence density = oil yield). This is the largest class.

### Class II — "Do not approach without the full protocol"

**Structural signature:** ⊙ = 𐑢 (non-critical), Ç
= 𐑤 (frozen-order), but without any preparative encoding in the morphology.
The form gives the name but withholds the instructions.

**What the plant is saying:** You are looking at me. I am not telling you what to do. The
knowledge you need is not on my surface. Come back when you have it from elsewhere.

**Exemplars:** Hellebore (beautiful, deadly, silent), Monkshood (hooded flower identifies but
does not instruct), Calamus (ploidy-toxicity invisible to morphology).

The silence is not a failure. It is the message. A plant with no trichome density pointer, no
readable bitter principle conditional, no Fibonacci winding counter is not communicating
poorly — it is communicating precisely: *no executable path exists here.* The tradition that
called these plants Saturnine, Martian, or otherwise malefic was reading this correctly. The
morphology does not invite. It terminates.

### Class III — "Bring a partner"

**Structural signature:** ɢ = 𐑠 (sequential coupling) but with an
underspecified second operand. The preparation instructions compile correctly but are
functionally incomplete without an additional agent not named in the plant's own encoding.

**What the plant is saying:** I open a socket. I do not name what you put in it. That
knowledge lives in the lineage, not in me.

**Exemplar:** *Banisteriopsis caapi* (ayahuasca vine). The vine contains MAO inhibitors which
inhibit the gut enzyme that metabolizes DMT before it can reach the brain. The vine's encoding
says: *I am an enabler. I am not the agent. Bring something.* It does not say what. *Psychotria
viridis* was supplied by the tradition, not by the vine's own channels.

The structural distinction from Class IV: the vine does not present a choice. It presents an
incomplete program with a placeholder. Filling the placeholder requires knowledge in the
mentor-mentee chain — which makes this statement class acutely vulnerable to the breaking of
that chain. Lose the lineage, lose the partner specification. The vine alone cannot tell you.

### Class IV — "The ceremony IS the medicine"

**Structural signature:** ɢ = 𐑜 (disjunctive coupling, XOR). The
plant presents a binary choice in the preparation itself. ⊙ = \odot but
the criticality is located in the Operator's transformation, not the plant's compound.

**What the plant is saying:** I present you with a gate. The passing through is the work. What
you carry out is not a compound — it is a decision you made while crossing.

**Exemplars:** *Lophophora williamsii* (peyote: eat dried buttons OR brew tea — two paths,
neither wrong, the choice structures the experience); *Nicotiana tabacum* in ceremonial use
(pipe OR chew OR insufflate — the preparation modality is the ritual frame); *Ricinus communis*
(ricin: poison OR immunotoxin — same compound, distinguished by delivery mechanism and
recipient).

The XOR gate does not produce medicine in the pharmaceutical sense. It produces a decision
space whose traversal is the therapeutic event. The Operator who passes through the disjunction
is not the same Operator who approached it.

### Class V — "Use me as-is"

**Structural signature:** Ω = 𐑷 (trivial winding). LOOP counter = 0.
The extraction cycle does not exist because the plant is already in its active state.

**What the plant is saying:** Do not process me. I am already what I need to be. Apply me
directly.

**Exemplar:** *Plantago major* (greater plantain). No trichome concentration gradient to
dereference. No Fibonacci winding to count. The leaf is applied to the wound as the leaf. The
medicine is at the surface. The morphology says: this is not a Class I plant asking to be
unlocked — this is a plant that is already open.

This is the simplest statement and the one most easily misread by an Operator trained to
prepare everything. The preparation would destroy the medicine. The instruction is: stop.

### Class VI — "I am timed — find the right phase"

**Structural signature:** Contradictory programs between morphological parts of the same plant,
dependent on developmental stage. The tuple changes across the lifecycle. Calendar knowledge
is required.

**What the plant is saying:** I am not always what you think I am. One part of me, at one
time, is medicine. Another part, at another time, is poison. The distinction is in the clock,
not in me.

**Exemplar:** *Colchicum autumnale*. Corm harvested in autumn: colchicine in usable
concentration, preparation path executable. Seeds in spring: contradictory alkaloid profile,
lethal dose at normal preparation quantities. Same plant. Different program. The encoding is
not lying — it is temporally indexed in a way that requires the Operator to know what month
it is.

The broader class includes any plant whose medicinal and toxic windows are lifecycle-dependent
rather than dose-dependent.

---

### Class VII — "I am addressed — check the recipient"

**Structural signature:** ɢ = 𐑜 (XOR disjunction) where the switch
is the Operator's identity and intent rather than the preparation method. Medicine OR poison;
the plant does not disambiguate. The Operator must.

**What the plant is saying:** My statement is conditional on who is receiving it. I do not know
which statement I am making until I know who you are.

**Exemplar:** *Ricinus communis* (also Class IV). Ricin: one of the most lethal proteins
known, extracted from the seed. Also the basis for immunotoxin cancer therapy — ricin coupled
to a cancer-specific antibody kills tumor cells with extraordinary precision. The same
compound. The switch is delivery mechanism and recipient cell type.

---

### Class VIII — "I am infrastructure — read me by part"

**Structural signature:** Different plant parts carry structurally distinct pharmaceutical
programs. No single tuple characterizes the whole plant. The Operator must address each organ
system separately.

**What the plant is saying:** I am not one medicine. I am several, each written on a different
part. Address the root, the leaf, the flower, and the seed separately — they will not give
you the same answer.

**Exemplars:** *Sambucus nigra* (elder: flowers → diaphoretic, berries → antiviral/immune,
bark → purgative, leaves → topical anti-inflammatory). *Taraxacum officinale* (dandelion:
leaf → diuretic, root → hepatic/cholagogue, flower → analgesic). Each organ system is a
separate structural address. The plant is a library, not a book.

## Ancillary Inquiry: Fungi and Animals

The *Phytoglyphica* framework was derived from plants — specifically, from plants whose
morphological features (leaf margin, trichome density, compound ratio, phyllotaxis, bitter
threshold) encode pharmaceutical instructions in the five channels. The question of whether
the same framework extends to fungi and animals is a structurally independent inquiry. The
extension is not by analogy but by the same five-channel logic operating on different
morphological substrates.

### Fungi

The five channels read differently in fungi because fungi do not photosynthesize and do not
have leaves. The morphological features that carry pharmaceutical instruction must be
re-identified:

| Plant channel | Botanical substrate | Fungal analogue |
|---|---|---|
| Bilateral serration (Ř) | Leaf margin | Cap margin regularity (entire vs. wavy vs. deeply incised) |
| Trichome density (Ç) | Glandular hair distribution | Pileal surface texture (smooth / viscid / fibrillose / scaly) |
| Compound leaf ratio (Γ) | Leaflet:rachis ratio | Stipe:cap diameter ratio |
| Bitter principle (Ħ) | Sesquiterpene lactone threshold | Amyloid reaction (Melzer's reagent: blue = starch-like polymers present) |
| Fibonacci phyllotaxy (Ω) | Leaf spiral divergence angle | Gill spacing Fibonacci count (edge-to-edge lattice structure of Agaricales) |

The amyloid reaction maps onto Ħ most naturally: the presence of complex polysaccharides that
produce a blue color in Melzer's reagent is the fungal equivalent of wormwood's blue
chamazulene — a visual verification that the winding has closed. In both cases, blue = Gate 3
confirmation. The gill lattice of Agaricales shows Fibonacci-consistent spacing in many
species; this would encode Ω in the same way that phyllotaxis does in plants.

### Insects

The morphological channels shift again for insects, but the five-channel logic persists:

| Plant channel | Insect analogue |
|---|---|
| Bilateral serration (Ř) | Wing venation bilateral symmetry |
| Trichome density (Ç) | Setal density on tarsi (contact-chemical sensing) |
| Compound ratio (Γ) | Compound eye facet density (spatial granularity of vision) |
| Bitter principle (Ħ) | Hemolymph alkaloid threshold (sequestered plant toxins — chirality carrier) |
| Fibonacci phyllotaxy (Ω) | Abdominal segment spiral patterning |

### Marine Invertebrates

The most structurally interesting extension. Marine invertebrates — particularly echinoderms
and molluscs — show the clearest Fibonacci and chirality encoding of any non-plant group. Sea
urchin spines trace a double-spiral lattice that is structurally identical to a sunflower
phyllotaxis (\Omega$). Nautilus shell chirality is the definitional case of a naturally fixed
Ħ value. Cone snail venom (*Conus* spp.) has a bitter-principle analogue in the
conotoxin peptide threshold — and the toxin specificity scales with shell chirality in a way
that directly parallels Axiom A (high chirality → kinetically arrested venom chemistry). These
are not analogies. They are instances of the same structural constraints operating in a
different morphological medium.

The full elaboration of these extensions — with derivations for specific species and
verification of cross-primitive axiom satisfaction — belongs to the next volume of this work.
The framework is in place. The extension is an application problem, not a theoretical one.
